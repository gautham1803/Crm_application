The 8 Agents
1. 🎯 LeadQualifierAgent
Simple explanation: The agent that decides if a new lead is worth your time.
What it does:

When a new contact/lead comes in, it immediately scores them
Uses BANT scoring — does this person have Budget, Authority, Need, and Timeline?
Reads everything in the CRM about them — company size, industry, revenue
Gives them a score like 72/100 and labels them Hot, Warm, or Cold
If Hot → automatically triggers NurturerAgent to draft a first email
Creates tasks for the rep like "Call this person today"

Real example:

Alex Johnson fills a form on your website. LeadQualifierAgent sees he works at a 500-person tech company, is a VP (decision maker), and mentioned "looking for CRM for Q2". Scores him 85/100 HOT. Immediately tells NurturerAgent to draft a first-touch email.

Autonomy: Fully automatic — runs without asking you anything

2. 🔍 ResearchAgent
Simple explanation: Your personal internet researcher that profiles every company and contact before you talk to them.
What it does:

When an account or contact is added, immediately researches them
Finds company news, recent funding, job postings, tech stack
Identifies growth signals — "they're hiring 10 salespeople = expansion"
Stores findings in AI memory so all future drafts reference this intel
Updates the account/contact record with everything it finds

Real example:

You add Meridian Healthcare as an account. ResearchAgent finds: "Meridian raised $40M Series B in March, recently posted VP of Sales role, uses Salesforce currently, 501-1000 employees." Now when NurturerAgent drafts an email to Robert Kim at Meridian, it references this context specifically.

Autonomy: Fully automatic — no human approval needed (it only reads, never sends anything)

3. 💌 NurturerAgent
Simple explanation: Your AI copywriter that writes personalized emails and SMS messages for every situation.
What it does:

Drafts first-touch cold outreach emails
Drafts follow-up emails after demos
Drafts re-engagement messages for stalled deals
Drafts post-close thank you + referral ask emails
Writes SMS check-ins
Uses ALL available context — deal stage, company research, last activity, days stalled — to make it personal
NEVER sends anything — always puts it in your Approvals inbox first

Real example:

Meridian deal stalled 14 days. DealOrchestratorAgent flags it. NurturerAgent drafts: "Hi Robert, Following our demo last week where David raised HIPAA compliance concerns — I wanted to share our security documentation specifically for healthcare organizations your size. Does Thursday work for a 30-min call?" — references the actual blocker, the actual person, the actual industry.

Autonomy: Drafts only — YOU must approve before anything sends

4. 👁️ OpportunityWatchAgent
Simple explanation: The agent that watches the news and tells you the perfect moment to reach out.
What it does:

Monitors public signals about your accounts — news, job postings, LinkedIn changes
Spots buying signals like "company just raised funding" or "just hired a new CTO"
Pings you when the timing is right to reach out
Does NOT draft anything itself — just surfaces the opportunity
Example alert: "GreenLeaf Manufacturing just posted 5 Sales Director roles — good time to reach out about scaling their CRM"

Real example:

You're targeting Atlas Financial Group. OpportunityWatchAgent notices they just announced expansion into 3 new markets. It alerts you: "🔔 Atlas Financial expanding — perfect time to reach out about CRM scaling." You then trigger NurturerAgent manually.

Autonomy: Fully automatic — just notifies you, no external actions

5. 📄 ProposalAgent
Simple explanation: Automatically generates a complete sales proposal document when a deal reaches the Proposal stage.
What it does:

When a deal moves to Proposal stage, generates a full proposal document
Pulls data from the deal — amount, products, line items, close date
Pulls account info — company name, industry, size
Uses your team's proposal template
Fills everything in automatically
Creates the document and attaches it to the deal
Does NOT send it — you download/share it yourself

Real example:

TechVista Enterprise License deal moves to Proposal stage. ProposalAgent generates: "Acufy Enterprise CRM Proposal for TechVista Solutions — 1x Enterprise Plan @ $95,400/year, includes implementation timeline, HIPAA compliance documentation, 6-week onboarding plan." Ready to attach to an email and send.

Autonomy: Fully automatic for document creation — no approval needed since it's internal

6. 🎯 DealOrchestratorAgent
Simple explanation: The agent that watches your pipeline and tells you what to do next on every deal.
What it does:

Monitors ALL deals in the pipeline constantly
Flags deals that are stuck or going cold
Analyzes deal health — healthy, at risk, stalled, critical
Suggests specific next actions — "send follow-up email", "schedule demo", "address pricing objection"
If a deal is stalled, automatically triggers NurturerAgent to draft a re-engagement email
Creates AI-proposed tasks for the rep
Tracks if documents have been collected, stakeholders engaged, etc.

Real example:

Meridian deal hasn't moved in 14 days. DealOrchestratorAgent flags it as STALLED, health score 42/100. Says: "Decision maker Robert Kim hasn't been contacted in 10 days. David Thompson (Influencer) raised security concerns that haven't been addressed. Recommend: send security documentation email + schedule technical call." Automatically triggers NurturerAgent.

Autonomy: Analysis is automatic — external communication still needs your approval

7. 📅 SchedulerAgent
Simple explanation: The agent that finds meeting times and drafts scheduling emails without you checking your calendar manually.
What it does:

When a demo or meeting needs to be scheduled, checks your Google Calendar
Finds 3-4 available slots that work for you
Drafts an email to the prospect with proposed times
Email goes into Approvals for you to review before sending
After you approve, creates the calendar event automatically
Handles the back-and-forth scheduling process

Real example:

DealOrchestratorAgent recommends scheduling a technical demo for Meridian. SchedulerAgent checks your calendar, finds you're free Thursday 2pm, Friday 10am, and next Monday 3pm. Drafts: "Hi Robert, I'd love to schedule a technical demo to address your team's security questions. I'm available Thursday at 2pm, Friday at 10am, or Monday at 3pm — does any of these work for you?" Goes to Approvals for your review.

Autonomy: Draft only — YOU approve the scheduling email before it sends

8. 🛡️ ComplianceAgent
Simple explanation: The legal guardrail that checks every single draft before it reaches your Approvals inbox — you cannot bypass it.
What it does:

Runs on EVERY draft before it becomes an approval
Checks 5 rules on every draft:

TCPA — Does this person have SMS consent? If not, BLOCKS the SMS
CAN-SPAM — Does the email have an unsubscribe link and physical address? If not, BLOCKS
GDPR/CCPA — Is there any PII being processed illegally? BLOCKS if yes
Anti-Discrimination — Does the message reference race, religion, gender, age, disability, or any protected class in a discriminatory way? BLOCKS if yes
Honesty — Are all claims grounded in real CRM data (prices, timelines, capabilities)? WARNS if something seems invented


If it blocks → draft never reaches you, you get an error message
If it warns → draft reaches you with a yellow warning, you must check a box to override
Cannot be disabled for TCPA and CAN-SPAM (these are laws)

Real example:

NurturerAgent drafts an SMS to Emily Watson. ComplianceAgent checks: Emily has NO SMS consent. BLOCKS immediately. You see: "Cannot create SMS approval — Emily Watson has revoked SMS consent." NurturerAgent never creates the approval. You'd need to email her instead.

Autonomy: Always blocking — runs automatically on EVERYTHING, no one can skip it

      ↓

      ↓

      ↓
ComplianceAgent → checks all 5 rules
      ↓
You → approve in inbox
      ↓
Email sends via SendGrid
      ↓
14 days later — no reply
      ↓
DealOrchestratorAgent → flags deal stalled
      ↓
NurturerAgent → drafts re-engagement email
      ↓
ComplianceAgent → checks again
      ↓
You → approve
      ↓
They reply — want a demo
      ↓

      ↓
You → approve

Demo happens, deal moves to Proposal

      ↓

      ↓
You → approve
      ↓
Deal won 🎉

      ↓
